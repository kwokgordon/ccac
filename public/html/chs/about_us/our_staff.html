Pastor Jeremy Wong

Young Adults and Community Outreach Pastor
jeremy.wong@calgarychinesealliance.org

 

Jeremy joined the Pastoral team in the summer of 2011, after graduating from Seminary with his Masters of Divinity. He has called Calgary Chinese Alliance Church his home church since high school and was born and raised in Calgary. Jeremy has a passion for journeying with young adults and growing in the knowledge and experience of Jesus Christ as Lord and Saviour. He has a heart for developing authentic community and building relationships with people in our surrounding community and in our city. He also has a love for creative expression and connecting the Gospel with all parts of life and society. He greatly enjoys the outdoors, with a passion for biking, basketball, soccer and tennis.

Rev. Jacob Lau

Cantonese Adult Pastor
jacob.lau@calgarychinesealliance.org
 

I met my wife Daisy in Hong Kong. We both love different kind of water sports, including swimming, kayaking, and windsurfing. And of course, we enjoy the company with others to taste different kinds of food.

In 1996, we responded to God’s calling to go to Costa Rica for eighteen months of short term mission work. Over there God renewed a lot of our perspectives, in particular the importance of missions.

Later on I came to Canada to further my study, and subsequently obtained a Bachelor degree in Christian Counseling, as well as a Master of Divinity degree, major in Pastoral Ministry with a minor in Missions. Due to our passion in missions, my wife and I joined the family of C&MA in 2009.

Rev. Alan Lam

Cantonese Youth Pastor
alan.lam@calgarychinesealliance.org 

 

Alan was born in a family of five with two younger sisters. As a young child, he lived in Hong Kong in a small apartment in Tuen Mun. He remembers his childhood having many friends and lots of fun. However, my family moved to Guatemala at the age of 13. I spent my time living cross-culturally and learned Spanish. Also, by the time of 19, I received Christ as my own Saviour.

After staying in Guatemala for 10 years, my family decided that I should pursue my further studies at Canada. I chose the University College of Cariboo (now is called Thompson River University) at Kamloops. I finished my degree major in computer science and mathematics, and also married my wife, Olga. During this five years, I have participated in International Ministry and sensed stronger and stronger every day God’s calling in in my life as a pastor. To walk in the road of faith, Olga and I decided to move to Calgary and studied at Ambrose Seminary.

Today, I am working as a pastor of youth and young adult at Calgary Chinese Alliance Church. With an additional family in my life called Arby, we are grateful to serve brothers and sisters of all ages, culture and languages. Particularly, with the young people speaking Cantonese, my goal is to motivate them to experience God deeper and deeper everyday and realize the amazing transformational power in the blood of Jesus Christ.

Pastor Jun Cai

Mandarin Ministry Pastor
jun.cai@calgarychinesealliance.org

 

Pastor Jun Cai was born and raised in Lanzhou, China. With her husband, Lixin Wang, and son, Daniel, who was less than one year old at the time, they immigrated to Calgary in June 2001. Three weeks later, Jun was touched by God’s love and received Jesus Christ in a Christian Conference. At the same time she was given a heart to live for Gospel.

Jun and her husband were baptized at Christmas 2002 at Calgary Chinese Alliance Church. Jun entered Canadian Theological Seminary in September 2003. After more than seven years of learning and doing ministries, Jun was called to serve as Mandarin Ministry Pastor at CCAC and has been in that position since January 2011. Seeking to live the motto, “Freely you have received, freely you give,” Pastor Jun has a passion to share the blessings of Gospel with others.
