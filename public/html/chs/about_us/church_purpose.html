Christ's Purposes for the Church

We can slice and dice them in different ways, but Christ’s purposes for church, as laid out in the New Testament, are unchanging and non-negotiable. We desire to allow these purposes to guide and inform everything we do as a congregation. The framework for Christ’s purposes for the church is the reality of God’s Kingdom, that in Jesus Christ, the living Lord of the church, God is seeking to reconcile all things to himself. And God has given to the church the ministry of reconciliation, to demonstrate and proclaim this reconciliation in its life and ministry together.

2 Corinthians 5.18-20: All this is from God, who reconciled us to himself through Christ, and has given us the ministry of reconciliation; 19that is, in Christ God was reconciling the world to himself, not counting their trespasses against them, and entrusting the message of reconciliation to us. 20So we are ambassadors for Christ, since God is making his appeal through us; we entreat you on behalf of Christ, be reconciled to God.

Ephesians 1.9-10: he has made known to us the mystery of his will, according to his good pleasure that he set forth in Christ, 10as a plan for the fullness of time, to gather up all things in him, things in heaven and things on earth.

The following six purposes all overlap and insect each other. They show how the church, as a fellowship of disciples following Jesus Christ, demonstrates and proclaims reconciliation:

 #1. The Church exists to express reconciliation and love for God through worship

Worship is our “love-language” toward God. We can express our love for God in daily living, in prayer, and in corporate worship together. Worship in the power of the Holy Spirit is a powerful tool in God’s toolbox to bring about life transformation, “until Christ is formed in you.” The church exists to nurture an environment where people can freely express their love for God in full surrender.

Gospel of John 4.23-24: But the hour is coming, and is now here, when the true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth, for the Father seeks such as these to worship him. 24God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth."

Ephesians 5.19-20: as you sing psalms and hymns and spiritual songs among yourselves, singing and making melody to the Lord in your hearts, 20giving thanks to God the Father at all times and for everything in the name of our Lord Jesus Christ.

#2. The Church exists to evangelize and baptize

We are called to make disciples of Jesus Christ and the path of discipleship begins long before a person trusts in Jesus Christ as Lord and Saviour, and is then baptized. Just as Jesus fully entered into the lives of people to draw them close to God’s mercy and forgiveness, so also we strive to be community that walks beside individuals, loving, and drawing them toward God’s love.

Matthew 28.19-20: Go therefore and make disciples of all nations, baptizing them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, 20and teaching them to obey everything that I have commanded you. And remember, I am with you always, to the end of the age."

1 Corinthians 9.16-18: If I proclaim the gospel, this gives me no ground for boasting, for an obligation is laid on me, and woe to me if I do not proclaim the gospel! 17For if I do this of my own will, I have a reward; but if not of my own will, I am entrusted with a commission. 18What then is my reward? Just this: that in my proclamation I may make the gospel free of charge, so as not to make full use of my rights in the gospel.

 #3. The Church exists to edify believers

Spirituality, spiritual formation, discipleship, sanctification – it has been called different things. But the idea is basically the same. As we go deep into the study of Scripture, in prayer, and in the Holy Spirit, and then faithfully, prayerfully, and truthfully apply to our lives the biblical teaching, keeping in step with the Holy Spirit, who is the very power that raised Jesus Christ from the dead, we are changed to be more like Jesus.

Galatians 4.19: My little children, for whom I am again in the pain of childbirth until Christ is formed in you,

Galatians 5.16, 22-23: Live by the Spirit … the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, generosity, faithfulness, 23gentleness, and self-control.

2 Timothy 3.16-17: All scripture is inspired by God and is useful for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness, 17so that everyone who belongs to God may be proficient, equipped for every good work.

#4. The Church exists to equip everyone for works of ministry

Christ has placed in each follower of Jesus a unique calling for ministry. And God has given to each believer spiritual gifts and abilities for service in the field of ministry that God has prepared for them. We prayerfully seek to help each other “clear away the clouds” and see the calling God has placed in each of our hearts and to discern and strengthen our areas of ministry giftedness. But that is not enough. We then seek to en-courage each other to let go of what needs letting go and in faithful obedience enter into the field of ministry prepared for each of us. And that field of ministry is always individuals into whom we can pour the love of Jesus Christ.

Ephesians 4.11-13: The gifts he gave were that some would be apostles, some prophets, some evangelists, some pastors and teachers, 12to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ, 13until all of us come to the unity of the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of God, to maturity, to the measure of the full stature of Christ.

1 Corinthians 12.4-7: Now there are varieties of gifts, but the same Spirit; 5and there are varieties of services, but the same Lord; 6and there are varieties of activities, but it is the same God who activates all of them in everyone. 7To each is given the manifestation of the Spirit for the common good.

Ephesians 2.10: For we are what he has made us, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand to be our way of life.

#5. The Church exists to express God’s love and reconciliation in fellowship with another

A group of friends following Jesus where Jesus leads is how the Gospel of John pictures the life of believers together. And Christ has given to us the gift of the Holy Spirit so that to some extent we can experience joyfully the very fellowship that God the Father and God the Son share in Glory. This fellowship in the Spirit is characterized by freedom from divisions, cliques, and clubbishness, where believers are quick to forgive and be forgiven, and where we love one another as Christ showed us how to love.

Gospel of Matthew 22.29: "You shall love your neighbor as yourself.”

Gospel of John 13.34-35: I give you a new commandment, that you love one another. Just as I have loved you, you also should love one another. 35By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you have love for one another."

1 Thessalonians 3.12: And may the Lord make you increase and abound in love for one another and for all, just as we abound in love for you.

Hebr 13.1: Let mutual love continue.

#6 The church exists to express God’s love and reconciliation in acts of compassion in the world

The salvation that Jesus offers is essentially liberation. Jesus is in the business of saving people by rescuing or setting them free from whatever constrains or hinders them from fully enjoying the blessings and love of God. In the Gospels we see Jesus continuously saving or releasing people from all kinds of situations. All who encounter Jesus can receive freely the grace of God. There is nothing and no one outside the scope of salvation.

Gospel of Matthew 25.34-40: Then the king will say to those at his right hand, "Come, you that are blessed by my Father, inherit the kingdom prepared for you from the foundation of the world;35for I was hungry and you gave me food, I was thirsty and you gave me something to drink, I was a stranger and you welcomed me, 36I was naked and you gave me clothing, I was sick and you took care of me, I was in prison and you visited me.' 37Then the righteous will answer him, "Lord, when was it that we saw you hungry and gave you food, or thirsty and gave you something to drink? 38And when was it that we saw you a stranger and welcomed you, or naked and gave you clothing? 39And when was it that we saw you sick or in prison and visited you?' 40And the king will answer them, "Truly I tell you, just as you did it to one of the least of these who are members of my family, you did it to me.'

Gospel of Luke 10.29-37: But wanting to justify himself, he asked Jesus, "And who is my neighbor?" 30Jesus replied, "A man was going down from Jerusalem to Jericho, and fell into the hands of robbers, who stripped him, beat him, and went away, leaving him half dead. 31Now by chance a priest was going down that road; and when he saw him, he passed by on the other side. 32So likewise a Levite, when he came to the place and saw him, passed by on the other side. 33But a Samaritan while traveling came near him; and when he saw him, he was moved with pity. 34He went to him and bandaged his wounds, having poured oil and wine on them. Then he put him on his own animal, brought him to an inn, and took care of him. 35The next day he took out two denarii, gave them to the innkeeper, and said, "Take care of him; and when I come back, I will repay you whatever more you spend.' 36Which of these three, do you think, was a neighbor to the man who fell into the hands of the robbers?" 37He said, "The one who showed him mercy." Jesus said to him, "Go and do likewise."

Galatians 5.1: For freedom Christ has set us free.
